Traffic Simulator

>




Speed Scaling Factor


Description

Experiment simulating the effects of driver curiosity on traffic patterns and congestion. Pertains to traffic conditions such as "rubber-necking" arising from external driver distraction that may affect driver behavior but does not physically restrict road/lane capacity.

Assumptions:


  • the closer the driver gets to the crash sight the quicker her curiostity will get satisfied
  • the more curious the the driver is the longer it will take to satisfy her curiosity
  • the faster the driver is going the slower her curiosity will be fulfilled

    Ci = tu*dc
    Cj = .5*tu + (tu^2)*dc
    where tu = time unit and dc = change in curiosity, Ci = .5 and Cj = 580
